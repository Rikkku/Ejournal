about me

Milourd Nicole Catayas

who am i?

I can never remember a moment in my childhood where I was not being babied. Maybe it’s because I’m the youngest of two siblings, but I don’t think that counts when I have as complicated a household as the one I currently have. To make this journal entry not take an hour to read; basically my parents left me and my sister to our grandmother’s care at age 4, alongside numerous cousins who have identical circumstances. Now imagine that but moving from numerous provinces in the Philippines; Kiamba, to Marbel, to Catmon, to Balamban, then finally, Cebu. And then Talisay. Perhaps it is because my grandmother’s feet itch when she’s not travelling or doing something. I guess I got that trait from her. I might have mentioned how I was being babied, but now that I think about it, it might have been due to my being smaller than my peers. In every flag ceremony, I would always be in front. I’d like to thank my mother’s mother for that; I had inherited the unlucky short gene. And the unlucky nearsighted gene. And the other unlucky well-endowed gene. Basically, I’m genetically a weird combination of a middle-aged woman and a child in middle school. Which honestly can explain me both physically and mentally. In school, my grandmother’s workaholic tendencies (having to work earnestly to afford her six children’s education) and my other grandmother’s passion for education (being a teacher and even hiking to indigenous peoples’ villages just to teach) resulted in my being an honor student. Well, that’s oversimplified. My own workaholic grandmother, who stays up until midnight taking care of the house, scolds me for staying late in school because of projects. And my other grandmother, who, though I only see once a year, keeps on commenting about how I haven’t grown in height. Calling the kettle black, you know?
