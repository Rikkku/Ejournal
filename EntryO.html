The Nature and Role of Educational and Developmental Psychology

I have always been fascinated by the Nature Vs. Nurture debate. Despite how many studies or research I find, this back and forth notion of your genetics and your environment becoming the determining factor which explains your development always divides me. I consider myself to be a pretty biased person, and when I have a certain belief I tend not to open my eyes to the whole picture. Until I learned how it stunted my growth. It might have been due to my nature of being hardheaded; or it might have been due to my grandmother’s upbringing, but it was only until a few years ago that I realized that my beliefs do not have to be stagnant.

Since researching my past and present behavior and just how different it is, I started seeing patterns. I would always get intimidated when people try to reprimand me for my behavior; and automatically think that they are against me, when it is really the opposite.

I was so defensive of myself and now that I am learning about human development, it dawns on me that the Nature Vs. Nurture debate is not a black and white spectrum; there is always a middle ground, the gray area. Our nature is affected by our nurture, and our nurture is affected by our nature. We are raised in different circumstances and households; and our personality determines our parents’ or guardians’ method of upbringing. In the past, I may have been biased about nurture being the determining factor of how one develops; but as I grow as a person and as a human, I realize everything is not black and white, and that there are numerous other shades that I have never realized I was seeing before.
