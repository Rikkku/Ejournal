Entry 1

Bio-physical processes

I never fully thought of myself as a nihilist until the quote “Everything happens for a reason” irked me. I thought, in a world as vast as ours, why would there be an explanation to every action? Until I realized when reading the sources regarding developmental theories that every single thing that happens to us in our lives contributes to who you are today. In all honesty, I had a slight existential crisis.

“Is the reason I’m only 4’9 because of genetics or because I might have chromosomal problems?” So I asked my mother.


She said, “No, Milourd, you’re healthy and normal.”


As I kept on reading the studies by numerous people, I remembered my mother’s mother and her sisters were all mainly 4 feet tall too. I sighed in relief but also got infuriated that I inherited the short gene.


This event made my resentment of the quote dissipate. If my genetic makeup can explain my complex regarding my height, it’s almost a given that anything and everything does have a meaning. Or, more precisely, a reason.


I still don’t believe in giving meaning to every situation, because it deescalates the fact that misfortunes in life are normal; and whether they were self-inflicted or not, it is still a normal phase in life to have events like that.

This is also true for my perception of positive happenings too, as I believe that it is not the work of a higher being that resulted from your hard work to accomplishments. I believe in the capacity of human trial-and-error, which is why my reassessment of my understanding of “Everything happens for a reason” is that of acceptance of the present because of the workings of the past.


Our current circumstance is an accumulation of the past, whether it is good or bad. Everything happens for a reason, and the reason is for our growth. Except for my height, I guess.
